WeTravel

Making travel planning easier with a community-based approach

May 2020

OVERVIEW

In my UX/UI Design bootcamp, our first project was to design an application that solved a problem around travel.

My team of 4 decided to create an app that made planning trips easier for novice and avid travelers alike.

ROLES

As UX/UI Bootcamp students, we all played a part in the whole design process - from user research, to paper sketches, and developing a clickable prototype

TOOLS

Figma, Miro, Adobe Photoshop

DURATION

4-week sprint

the problem

Planning a trip can be, and usually is, a hassle. You might spend countless hours surfing the web, looking for the best prices at the best destinations, especially when you want your vacation to be a memorable one.

But us travelers are prone to misleading marketing from booking websites and applications that fail to meet our expectations.

There are not enough real photos and reviews from real, verified travelers

user research

We started off by each interviewing 5 users for a total of 20 interviews. A lot of the responses we received then helped us make more focused survey questions.

Our target audience ranged from avid travellers to the once-a-year vacation goer.

Once we’ve collected the data we needed, we put on our Zoom pants and collaborated in a Miro board. Making the I Like... I Wish... What if... matrix helped us find a common theme:

People like to get recommendations and copy itineraries from travel bloggers, because it’s reliable.
